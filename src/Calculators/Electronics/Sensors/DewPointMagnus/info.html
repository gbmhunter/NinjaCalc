This calculator uses the August-Roche-Magnus equation (also known just as Magnus equation) to allow the calculation of either air temperature, relative humidity, or the dew point (as a temperature, also known as the dew point temperature), when you know the other two values.

The dew point is given as:

$$ T_{dp} = \frac{c*(ln(RH) + \frac{b \cdot T_{air}}{c \cdot T_{air}})}{b - ln(RH) - \frac{b \cdot T_{air}}{c \cdot T_{air}}} $$

where:
\( T_{dp} \) = the dew point temperature, in degrees Celcius
\( RH \) = The relative humidity, as a number between 0-1 (i.e. percentage divided by 100)
\( b \) = A Magnus equation coefficient, which has no unit
\( c \) = A Magnus equation coefficient, in degrees Celcius

The exact values for the Magnus equation coefficients \(b\) and \(c\) depend on the exact literature that you the equation from. Common values for both are:

\( b = 17.625 \)
\( c = 243.03^{\circ}C \)
