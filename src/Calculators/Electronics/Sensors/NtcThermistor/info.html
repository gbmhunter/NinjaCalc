This calculator allows the calculation of various thermistor properties by using the Beta equation. The Beta equation is commonly used in microcontroller code to work out the thermistor temperature after the micro's ADC measures the thermistors resistance.

The beta equation is:

$$ \frac{1}{T} = \frac{1}{T_0} + \frac{1}{\beta} ln (\frac{R}{R_0}) $$

where:
\( \beta \) is the Beta co-efficient. This is usually specified in the thermistors datasheet.
\( T_0 \) is the temperature at the reference point, in Kelvin. This is usually \(25^{\circ}C\).
\( R_0 \) is the resistance at the reference point, in Ohms.
\( T \) is the thermistor temperature, with measured resistance \( R \), in Kelvin.
\( R \) is the thermistor resistance, measured at temperature \(T\), in Ohms.

This equation can be re-arranged to calculate any of the variables when the other 4 are known.

To find \(\beta\):

$$ \beta = \frac{ln(\frac{R}{R_0})}{\frac{1}{T} - \frac{1}{T_0}} $$

To find \(R_0\):

$$ R_0 = \frac{R}{e^{\beta (\frac{1}{T} - \frac{1}{T_0})}} $$

To find \(T_0\).

$$ T_0 = \frac{1}{\frac{1}{T} - \frac{1}{\beta}ln(\frac{R}{R_0})} $$

To find \(R\):

$$ R = R_0 e^{\beta (\frac{1}{T} - \frac{1}{T_0})} $$

To find \(T\):

$$ T = \frac{1}{\frac{1}{T_0} + \frac{1}{\beta}ln(\frac{R}{R_0})} $$
